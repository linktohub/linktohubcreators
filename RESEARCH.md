## Competitor Intelligence

### Stan.store
- **Pricing:** $29/mo (Creator) | $99/mo (Creator Pro). No free plan, 14-day trial only.
- **Fees:** 0% platform fee. Only Stripe/PayPal processing (2.9% + $0.30).
- **What's included at $29:** Digital products, courses, bookings, subscriptions, community, AutoDM. No email marketing.
- **What's locked at $99:** Email marketing, funnels, upsells, discount codes, affiliate tools, pixel tracking, payment plans.
- **Weaknesses:** Template-heavy store (minimal visual differentiation between stores). Course tools have no quizzes, certificates, or progress tracking. No free tier = barrier for pre-revenue creators. Email being $99 is the loudest complaint across every review site.
- **Trustpilot:** 4.8/5 from 1,906 reviews — strong brand reputation to beat.
- **Conversion hook:** "Your audience is waiting. Start selling in minutes."

### Beacons.ai
- **Pricing:** Free (9% fee) | Creator $10/mo (9% fee) | Store Pro $30/mo (0% fee) | Business Pro $90/mo.
- **Key AI feature:** "Beam" — AI teammate for analytics, brand deal strategy, content, captions. Smart Reply AutoDM.
- **Strong features:** Media kit auto-builder for brand deals, geo-targeted campaigns, NFC card (Business tier), Google Workspace unlock.
- **Free tier is aggressively generous:** Unlimited digital products, email (50/mo), automations (5), real-time analytics — all at 9% fee.
- **Weakness:** 9% fee on free and Creator tiers is brutal at scale. $1k/mo in sales = $90 just to Beacons, before Stripe fees. Also does not have merch/POD integration.
- **Conversion hook:** "All-in-one creator platform" with AI.

### Gumroad
- **Pricing:** No monthly fee. 10% flat platform fee + $0.50 per transaction + 2.9% + $0.30 Stripe = effectively ~12.9% + $0.80 per sale.
- **Discover marketplace:** 30% fee — avoid recommending this to creators.
- **Important 2025 change:** Full Merchant of Record since January 2025 — handles all global tax (VAT, GST, sales tax). This is a genuine pain-removal for international sellers.
- **Weakness:** At $10k/mo revenue = $1,000/mo to Gumroad. No subscription/membership tools. No bookings. No merch. No link-in-bio. Single product type focus.
- **Conversion hook:** No monthly fee, just sell.

### Linktree
- **Pricing (raised late 2025):** Free (12% sales fee) | Starter $8/mo (9% fee) | Pro $15/mo (9% fee, email integrations) | Premium $35/mo.
- **Critical weakness:** Free tier takes 12% per sale — worst fee structure of any competitor. Prices raised ~40-50% in late 2025 without major feature additions.
- **Complaints from users:** No email capture by default. Safety researchers flagged platform for hosting phishing/spam links — brand-risk for verified creators. Missing autosave causing lost work. Shallow analytics. No monetization tools beyond basic links.
- **What it does well:** Brand recognition, simplicity, huge install base.
- **Conversion hook:** Still #1 brand in the category — relies on name recognition.

### Koji
- **Pricing:** Free. Takes 5–15% of transactions through Withkoji apps.
- **Features:** 300+ mini-apps for audience interaction (tip jar, shoutout, paid DMs, NFT minting). Apps open inside social media apps natively.
- **Status warning:** Multiple sources indicate Koji may be pivoting or winding down its current product. Low priority competitor — monitor but don't over-index on.

## Creator Pain Points

1. **Email marketing gated behind expensive tiers** (Stan $99, Linktree $15+) — "I left Stan because I can't afford $99 just to send emails to my own list." (Seen across Trustpilot, Reddit r/sidehustle, and review blogs.) Email is the highest-ROI channel for digital product sales and creators resent it being paywalled.

2. **High percentage fees eating into early-stage revenue** — Gumroad's 10% + Linktree's 12% hurt most at sub-$2k/mo revenue when margins matter most. Pre-revenue creators can't absorb this. Multiple comparison articles explicitly frame this as "the Gumroad/Linktree tax."

3. **Platform dependency / not owning your audience** — Instagram algorithm shifts in 2024-2025 made follower count nearly meaningless. A Creator.com 2026 survey found 67% of successful creators use 3+ revenue streams. Creators are actively looking for tools that help them build owned audiences (email lists, communities), not just link traffic.

4. **Thin course/education features** — No quizzes, no certificates, no drip scheduling (Stan). Creators selling courses feel forced to pay Kajabi ($119/mo) or Teachable just for a completion certificate. This is widely cited in "Stan Store alternatives" searches.

5. **Poor customization / template-heavy stores** — Stan stores all look identical. Linktree themes are limited on free/starter. Creators building a brand want visual differentiation.

6. **No merch integration anywhere except niche tools** — Beacons, Stan, Linktree don't offer POD merch. Creators have to maintain a separate Printify/Gelato account and manually link out. This is a friction point Linktohub directly solves.

## Feature Ideas (ranked by impact)

1. **Email marketing at mid-tier ($29–39/mo)** — Directly converts Stan Pro users. Include sequences, automations, and audience segmentation. Even basic email beats having nothing. Win condition: "Stan does this for $99; we do it for $29."

2. **Course certificates + quiz builder** — Low build complexity, high perceived value. Certification is the #1 missing feature in sub-$50 creator course tools. Target: fitness, business, cooking, and education creators who sell $97–$497 courses.

3. **AI voice assistant demo page + creator case study** — The feature already exists. It needs a 60-second demo video with a real creator example. "Your store answers your followers' questions, in your voice, 24/7." No competitor does this.

4. **Merch + digital bundle upsells** — Let creators bundle a digital download with a Gelato merch item at checkout. No competitor offers this. "Buy my preset pack, get the branded hoodie at 20% off." High AOV, zero extra effort for creator.

5. **Media kit generator for brand deals** — Beacons has this and it drives creator sign-ups because it solves a real pain (brands ask for media kits, creators scramble). Auto-populate from Linktohub analytics. Could be a top-of-funnel acquisition feature.

6. **AutoDM integration (Instagram/TikTok)** — Stan and Beacons both offer this. Creators drive followers to DM a keyword, which triggers the AutoDM with their store link. High conversion. Currently missing from Linktohub.

7. **Instant payouts toggle** — Stripe now supports embedded Instant Payouts UI components (settled within 30 minutes). Creators hate waiting 7 days. "Get paid instantly" is a real conversion hook, especially against Gumroad's weekly payout cycle.

## Tech Opportunities

**Stripe Instant Payouts (act now):**
Stripe released embedded Instant Payouts UI components — platforms can surface this natively inside the product. Funds settle in ~30 minutes. Fee is a small %, but creators love it. Implement as an opt-in toggle. Direct win vs. Gumroad's slow payout cycle.

**Stripe Stablecoins + 135 currencies:**
Stripe's creator economy stack now supports stablecoin payouts and 135+ currencies for cross-border payments. Opportunity for Linktohub to attract international creators and position as "global from day one."

**Printify as Gelato backup/expansion:**
Printify offers 1,300+ products vs. Gelato's ~250. Gelato is strong on global fulfillment speed, but creators want catalog breadth. Consider offering Printify as an optional second POD provider, or specifically for the US market where Printify's network is stronger.

**Fourthwall POD model to study:**
Fourthwall is creator-first POD + store in one — gaining traction with YouTube/Twitch creators. Study their onboarding and store presentation for UX inspiration. They're the closest POD competitor to Linktohub's merch vision.

**AI Agent market (46.3% YoY growth):**
The AI agent market is growing from $7.84B (2025) to projected $52.62B (2030). Creator-voice AI assistants are not yet commoditized. Linktohub's AI trained on creator voice is ahead of the curve — protect and extend this lead before Beacons or Stan copies it.

**Instagram/TikTok AutoDM APIs:**
Both Stan and Beacons offer AutoDM. It's a proven conversion driver (follower DMs keyword → gets store link automatically). This integration is now table-stakes for the category. Prioritize for Q3.
