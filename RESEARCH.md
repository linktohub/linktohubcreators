## Competitor Intelligence

### Stan.store
- **Pricing:** $29/mo (Creator) | $99/mo (Creator Pro). No free plan, 14-day trial only. 0% platform fee on both tiers.
- **Jan 2026 launch: AutoDM** — sends a message/download when a follower DMs or comments a trigger word. Significant conversion driver. Now heavily featured in Stan's own marketing and "how to monetize Instagram" content.
- **Locked at $99 (Creator Pro):** Email marketing, funnels, affiliates, upsells, pixel tracking, payment plans, discount codes.
- **Course weakness persists:** No quizzes, no certificates, no progress tracking. Creators needing these still must go to Kajabi ($119/mo) or Teachable.
- **Revenue milestone:** $500M+ paid to creators (now on homepage). Strong social proof.
- **Conversion hook:** "Your audience is waiting. Start selling in minutes."
- **Verdict:** Strongest direct competitor. AutoDM launch this year narrows Linktohub's feature gap; email at $99 remains their biggest exposed flank.

### Beacons.ai
- **Pricing:** Free (9% fee) | Creator $10/mo (9% fee) | Creator Plus $30/mo (0% fee) | Creator Max $90/mo.
- **Scale:** 8M+ users, ~$10.8M ARR. Fastest-growing platform in the category.
- **AI differentiator: "Beam"** — AI teammate for analytics, brand deal strategy, captions, content optimization. Can answer "why did my follower count spike last week?"
- **Smart Reply AutoDM** available at all tiers.
- **Media kit auto-builder** — pulls stats to generate brand deal pitches automatically. Drives creator sign-ups because brands ask for media kits constantly.
- **Critical weakness:** 9% fee is brutal at scale ($1k/mo in sales = $90 to Beacons before Stripe fees). Fee doesn't drop to 0% until $30/mo Creator Plus — and at that tier, full email automations are still limited.
- **Still no merch/POD integration.** Creators must link out to Printify/Gelato separately.
- **Conversion hook:** "All-in-one creator platform" with AI — directly competing with Linktohub's positioning.

### Gumroad
- **Pricing:** No monthly fee. 10% flat platform fee + $0.50/transaction. Stripe processing adds ~2.9% + $0.30. Effective take rate: ~12.9% + $0.80 per sale.
- **Discover marketplace fee:** 30% — a tax on discoverability that creators increasingly avoid.
- **Genuine strength:** Full Merchant of Record since January 2025 — handles VAT, GST, and sales tax globally. Huge pain removal for international digital product sellers.
- **Hard limitations:** No bookings, no memberships, no events, no merch, no link-in-bio, no AutoDM, no email marketing. Pure single-product-sale focus.
- **Verdict:** Low direct threat. Attracts creators who just want a checkout link and don't need a storefront. Linktohub beats it on every dimension except simplicity and the $0/mo entry point.

### Linktree
- **Pricing (raised Nov 2025):** Free (12% sales fee) | Starter $8/mo (9% fee) | Pro $15/mo (9% fee) | Premium $35/mo (0% fee).
- **The Koji acquisition (Dec 2023) is now baked in:** Koji's 400+ mini-apps are being absorbed into Linktree's commerce layer — interactive tip jars, paid DMs, fan subscriptions. This strengthens Linktree's commerce story but remains under-marketed.
- **Structural weakness:** Still just a routing layer. No email capture on free or Starter. No CRM. No AutoDM. No merch. Traffic hits the page, clicks a link, disappears. Linktree has no memory of the visitor.
- **Price hike backlash:** A 67% Pro price increase with no new headline features has "Linktree alternatives 2026" searches elevated. Creators feel held hostage by brand recognition, not features.
- **Conversion hook:** Relies entirely on category name recognition — "link in bio."

### Koji
- **Status confirmed:** Acquired by Linktree December 2023. Operating as part of Linktree's commerce expansion. No longer an independent competitor. Monitor Linktree's mini-app rollout instead.

## Creator Pain Points

1. **AutoDM absence = lost conversions.** Instagram's comment-to-DM automation is confirmed to convert 3–5x better than link-in-bio clicks. Creators using Stan/Beacons for AutoDM report it as their #1 revenue driver. Creators who want it but don't have it yet are actively looking for platforms that offer it. (Source: CreatorFlow comparison data, Instagram API documentation May 2026.)

2. **Email locked behind expensive tiers.** Stan's $99/mo Pro for email is the single most-cited reason creators search for alternatives in 2026. "I left Stan because I can't afford $99/mo just to email my own list" appears across Trustpilot, Reddit r/sidehustle, and every independent review published this year.

3. **High percentage fees are brutal pre-revenue.** Linktree's 12% free tier fee, Gumroad's 10% + $0.50, and Beacons' 9% all punish creators at the $500–$2k/month stage when margins matter most. A creator making $1,500/mo on Linktree free hands over $180/mo to the platform before Stripe fees.

4. **Platform dependency / algorithm fragility.** Instagram's 2024-2025 algorithm changes made follower count "nearly meaningless" (multiple sources). Meta's Creator Fast Track ($1k–$3k/mo to lure creators to Facebook) is creating urgency for owned-audience tools: email lists, direct checkout, platform-neutral storefronts. 67% of successful creators now use 3+ revenue streams.

5. **No all-in-one solution exists below $90/mo.** Patreon lacks: paid DMs, 1-on-1 video calls, messaging automation, merch, e-commerce storefront. Stan lacks: email (under $99), quizzes, certificates, merch. Beacons lacks: merch/POD. Linktree lacks: everything except links. The $25–$45/mo slot with 0% fees + email + merch + bookings is genuinely empty.

6. **Course tools are inadequate under $119/mo.** Sub-$50 platforms (Stan, Beacons) offer no quizzes, no certificates, no drip scheduling. Creators selling $97–$497 educational courses feel forced toward Kajabi or Teachable. Certificate issuance is the #1 most-cited missing feature.

## Feature Ideas (ranked by impact)

1. **AutoDM (Instagram)** — Trigger a DM with a store link, download, or coupon when a follower comments a keyword on a Reel/post. Instagram API supports this. Stan and Beacons both have it. Converts 3–5x better than link-in-bio. Ship this sprint or next. TikTok version not possible yet (API restriction confirmed May 2026).

2. **Email marketing included in base plan** — Make $29–39/mo include sequences, automations, and audience segmentation. Win condition vs. Stan: "We do what Stan Pro ($99/mo) does for your email list, at our base price." This is the highest-ROI positioning move available right now.

3. **Meta/multi-platform creator landing page** — "Built for creators who post everywhere." Facebook's Creator Fast Track is actively moving creators onto multi-platform strategies. A dedicated acquisition page targeting this cohort with Linktohub's neutral-storefront message has no direct competition.

4. **Direct Linktree comparison page** — "Linktree Premium: $35/mo + links only. Linktohub [tier]: $[X]/mo + merch + digital products + events + bookings + memberships + AI assistant." This is a facts-based takedown that writes itself and captures high-intent switchers.

5. **Course certificates + quiz builder** — Low dev complexity, high perceived value. Certification is the #1 missing feature in sub-$50 course tools. Target fitness, business, cooking, and education creators selling $97–$497 courses.

6. **Media kit auto-generator** — Pull Linktohub analytics into a one-click branded PDF for brand deal pitches. Beacons uses this as a top-of-funnel acquisition feature. Solves a real, recurring creator pain (brands ask for media kits; creators scramble).

7. **Merch + digital bundle upsells** — Bundle a digital download with a Gelato merch item at checkout. "Buy my preset pack, get the branded hoodie at 20% off." No competitor offers cross-category bundling. High AOV, zero extra creator effort.

## Tech Opportunities

**Stripe Accounts v2 API (Dec 2025 launch):**
Stripe's new Accounts v2 replaces rigid account types with flexible "configurations" (merchant, customer, recipient). An account can hold multiple roles simultaneously. For Linktohub: cleaner payout flows for creators, easier multi-currency setup, and a path to embedded financial services (wallets, lending) down the road. Start new Connect integrations on v2.

**Stripe Instant Payouts (already available):**
Creators hate waiting 7 days for payouts (Gumroad's default). Stripe's Instant Payouts settle in ~30 minutes, 24/7. Expose this as an opt-in toggle in the creator dashboard. Direct win vs. Gumroad's slow cycle. Small fee (0.25% capped at $25) passed to creator is accepted willingly.

**Printify as Gelato supplement:**
Printify offers 1,300+ customizable products; Gelato offers ~250. Gelato is stronger on international/EU fulfillment speed. Printify has deeper US catalog breadth. Offer Printify as an optional second POD provider — especially useful for US-based creators wanting product variety. Podbase (24-hour US/EU printing) is also worth evaluating for speed positioning.

**Instagram AutoDM API (proven, ready to use):**
Instagram's official API fully supports comment-triggered DM automation. Stan and Beacons have already integrated this. There is no technical barrier — this is a sprint-scoped integration, not a quarter-long project.

**TikTok DM API (not available):**
TikTok's developer documentation does NOT expose DM sending/receiving to third parties as of May 2026. Do not roadmap TikTok AutoDM — it will create false expectations. Instagram-only is the correct and honest scope.

**AI creator tools are growing but not differentiated:**
85% of marketers now use AI tools. Faceless content grew from 12% to 38% of new monetization ventures in 3 years. The AI tools market is crowded at the content-creation layer (Midjourney, HeyGen, 11Labs) but thin at the creator-commerce layer. Linktohub's AI assistant trained on creator voice is genuinely differentiated — no competitor has a commerce-facing, voice-matched AI. This needs marketing, not more development.

**Facebook Creator Fast Track (March 2026):**
Meta launched a program paying $1,000/mo to 100k+ follower creators and $3,000/mo to 1M+ creators to post on Facebook. This signals that the multi-platform creator is the dominant archetype now — creators will not consolidate to one social platform. They need a commerce layer that sits above all platforms. Linktohub's single-link storefront is structurally positioned for this; market it that way.
